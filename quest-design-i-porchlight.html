Do I really need official plans for a small project?+

For almost anything with walls, a roof, or wiring — yes. Cities in the Valley require approved plans before they'll issue a permit, and building without one can mean fines or being forced to undo the work. The good news: for small projects, the plan set is small too.

Do I need to hire an architect?+

For most houses, additions, and casitas in Arizona — no. Residential projects like these don't require an architect's license, just well-drawn plans the city accepts. That's exactly what we make. If your project ever does need an engineer's stamp (some structural work does), we coordinate that for you.

How long does the whole thing take?+

Design time depends on how quickly we land on a version you love. Drawing the official set typically takes a few weeks, and the city's review takes a few more — it varies by city and season. You'll get an honest timeline with your quote, and we'll keep you posted at every step.

What if the city rejects something?+

Deep breath — it's normal, and it's not a rejection. Reviewers send back a list of corrections on almost every project, even perfect ones. We answer every comment and revise the plans at no extra charge, as many rounds as it takes, until you're approved.

Do you build the house too?+

We draw; builders build. That split actually protects you — our plans are detailed enough that any quality builder can give you an accurate price, and you're free to choose whoever you trust. We're happy to talk through your set with your builder, too.

What does it cost?+

It depends on the size and type of project, so we quote each one individually — for free, usually within two business days. The number you get is flat and includes everything: design, 3D previews, the full plan set, and all the city back-and-forth.
