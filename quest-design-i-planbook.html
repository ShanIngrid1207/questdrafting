CHAPTER I

What you receive

Four things, in order
No. 1

The design

Tell us how your family lives and what the house should do better. We shape the rooms around your answers — and around your lot's rules, which we look up before we draw anything, so there are no surprises at City Hall.

In the trade, this is called architectural design.
No. 2

The preview

A lifelike picture of your home before it exists — the kitchen at supper time, the casita from the back porch. If a room feels tight, we widen it on paper, where it costs nothing.

In the trade: 3-D visualization.
No. 3

The plans

The complete instruction manual for the build: every wall, beam and outlet, measured and drawn to today's building code. This is the set the city reviews and your builder prices.

In the trade: construction documents.
No. 4

The permit

We file the plans with your city, answer every question their reviewers raise, and revise until the permit is approved. There is no extra charge for this, however many rounds it takes.

In the trade: permit stewardship.

CHAPTER V

Questions from the kitchen table

Asked on nearly every project

Do I really need official plans for a small project?

For nearly anything with walls, a roof or wiring, yes — Valley cities require approved plans before they issue a permit, and building without one risks fines or tearing the work out. The good news: for a small project, the plan set is small too, and so is the price.

Do I need to hire an architect?

For most houses, additions and casitas in Arizona, no. Residential projects like these don't require an architect's license — just well-drawn plans the city accepts, which is precisely what we make. When a project needs an engineer's stamp for structural work, we arrange that and fold it into the quote.

How long does the whole thing take?

Design takes as long as it takes you to love it. Drawing the full set runs a few weeks; the city's review runs a few more, varying by city and season. Your written quote comes with an honest timeline, and we report progress at every step.

What happens if the city rejects something?

It isn't a rejection — it's a list of questions, and almost every project gets one, including excellent ones. We answer each comment and revise the plans at no charge, as many rounds as it takes, until the permit is approved.

Do you build the house too?

We draw; builders build. The split works in your favor: our plans are detailed enough that any good builder can price them accurately, so you're free to hire whoever you trust — and we're glad to walk your builder through the set.

What does it cost?

It depends on the size and kind of project, so we quote each one individually — free, in writing, usually within two business days. The number covers design, previews, the full plan set, and all the back-and-forth with the city.
