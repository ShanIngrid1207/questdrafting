One studio, six ways to say it

Every direction shares the Quest voice: serif elegance, drafting precision, and real project imagery. They differ in mood, energy, and how far they push interaction. Hover a preview to interact with it live, or open any design full screen.

G

Odyssey · the director's cut

from dirt to done, one unbroken shot
Open
Click to interact — then scroll the film

The scroll is a film. It opens on an empty desert lot under the stars; survey lines draw themselves in the dirt; the camera dives through the front door as the house drafts itself around you, joist by joist; the walls turn solid as dawn breaks; the city stamp slams down with a screen shake; and the wireframe dissolves into the finished home. One continuous 3D shot, with cinematic letterboxing, a film-act timeline, and sound design on the stamp.

Plan Book

a home-plan catalog for regular folks
Open
Click to interact

The non-technical read, styled as a mid-century home-plan catalog — with one magic trick: the hero is a draggable line where an ink sketch of a real Quest home becomes the finished photograph. Drag it and you've understood the whole business. Print rules instead of cards, a dotted-leader contents page that draws itself, numbered plan entries, an "APPROVED" stamp that slams onto the price box, and a fully visible kitchen-table Q&A.
